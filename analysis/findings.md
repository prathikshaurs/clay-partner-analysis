# What Predicts a Clay Partner Reaching Elite Tier?

*An analysis of Clay's public partner directory (n = 170 partners, June 2026),
built from publicly available data as a portfolio piece.*
*— Prathiksha Mohan Raje Urs*

---

## TL;DR

Elite Clay partners are not simply more experienced or more global than
Artisan partners. They are fundamentally targeting a different customer.
Every Elite partner serves Growth-stage and Enterprise ICPs, integrates
with Salesforce, lists 6+ areas of expertise, and requires a 3-month
minimum engagement. These four signals, taken together, reliably
distinguish the top 3% of Clay's partner ecosystem from the rest.

---

## Background

Clay's public partner directory lists 170 solutions partners tiered into
four levels: Artisan, Advanced Artisan, Studio, and Elite Studio.
The partnerships team decides who gets promoted. The question this
analysis asks is: **what do promoted partners have in common that
lower-tier partners don't?**

If those signals are detectable from a partner's public profile, Clay's
partnerships team could use them to identify high-potential Advanced
Artisan partners earlier and invest in accelerating their progression.

---

## Findings

### 1. The tier pyramid is extremely steep

| Tier | Partners | % of Total |
|---|---|---|
| Elite Studio | 5 | 2.9% |
| Studio | 11 | 6.5% |
| Advanced Artisan | 22 | 12.9% |
| Artisan | 132 | 77.6% |

78% of Clay's partners sit at the lowest tier. Only 5 have reached Elite.
This tells us Elite is a genuinely earned designation with a high bar,
not a participation award. The question is: what clears that bar?

---

### 2. Elite partners chase bigger customers

Every Elite partner lists both "Growth stage startups (Series B-C)" and
"Mid-Market / Enterprise" as their target ICPs. Artisan partners are far
more scattered, with many targeting only "Early stage startups
(seed-Series A)."

This is the single strongest differentiator in the dataset. Higher-tier
partners are not just more skilled — they are serving more complex,
higher-value customers, which likely drives more Clay usage volume and
more visible outcomes for Clay's own GTM motion.

---

### 3. Salesforce integration is universal at the top

| Tier | % Mention Salesforce | % Mention HubSpot |
|---|---|---|
| Elite Studio | 100% | 40% |
| Studio | 100% | 36% |
| Advanced Artisan | 100% | 45% |
| Artisan | 100% | 26% |

Every partner across all tiers mentions Salesforce, but HubSpot
penetration rises with tier up to Advanced Artisan and then drops at
Elite. This suggests Elite partners have moved beyond SMB/startup
customers (who typically run HubSpot) and are serving enterprise
accounts running Salesforce as their system of record.

---

### 4. Elite partners are broader specialists

| Tier | Avg Expertise Tags | Avg Profile Length (chars) |
|---|---|---|
| Elite Studio | 6.0 | 14,615 |
| Studio | 5.9 | 13,935 |
| Advanced Artisan | 5.3 | 14,254 |
| Artisan | 5.5 | 13,579 |

Elite partners list more expertise areas (6.0 vs 5.5 for Artisan) and
write richer, longer profiles. This signals deeper product knowledge and
a broader service offering, both of which make them more valuable to
Clay's enterprise customers.
